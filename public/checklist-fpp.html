Property Damage
Claim Checklist

Protect your rights after hurricane, water, fire, or roof damage in Florida

0 completed

Immediately After Damage
Document Everything
✓
Take photos and video of ALL damage — interior, exterior, roof, belongings
Use your phone's timestamp. Take wide shots AND close-ups of every affected area.
✓
Do not throw away damaged items until your claim is fully resolved
The insurance company has the right to inspect. Disposing of items can void your claim.
✓
Make emergency repairs ONLY to prevent further damage
Keep ALL receipts. You are entitled to reimbursement for reasonable emergency measures.
✓
Save receipts for ALL expenses — temporary repairs, hotel, food, storage
✓
Do NOT make permanent repairs until the insurance company has inspected
✓
Write down what happened while details are fresh
Contact Your Insurance Company
✓
Report the claim immediately
Under F.S. 627.70131, insurers must acknowledge your claim within 14 days.
✓
Get your claim number, adjuster's name, and direct contact info
✓
Request a complete copy of your policy
Your policy contains coverage details, exclusions, and conditions the carrier must follow.
✓
Ask about Additional Living Expenses (ALE) if home is uninhabitable
Coverage D pays for temporary housing, food above normal costs, storage, and more.
✓
Do NOT give a recorded statement without consulting an attorney
Anything you say can be used to minimize or deny your claim.
✓
Do NOT sign a proof of loss or release without legal review
During the Claim Process
Protect Your Claim
✓
Get your own independent repair estimate from a licensed contractor
Insurance company estimates are almost always lower than actual repair costs.
✓
Keep a log of every call, email, and interaction with your insurer
Note the date, time, who you spoke with, and what was said.
✓
Do NOT accept the first offer without having it reviewed
First offers are typically 30-50% below what the claim is actually worth.
✓
Review the adjuster's estimate line by line — flag anything missing
✓
Check if your policy includes Ordinance & Law coverage
If your home was built before 2002, code upgrades can add tens of thousands to repair costs.
✓
Know your deadlines — 2-year statute of limitations for property claims
✓
Request an appraisal if you disagree with the carrier's valuation
Most Florida policies include an appraisal clause — often faster than litigation.
If Denied or Underpaid
✓
Get the denial in writing — carrier must state the specific reason
✓
Request a re-inspection if the adjuster missed damage
✓
File a Civil Remedy Notice (F.S. 624.155)
Required pre-suit step before you can file a bad faith action in Florida.
✓
Hire a property damage attorney — most work on contingency
✓
Do NOT let the statute of limitations expire while waiting
Insurance companies count on delay. Every day weakens your position.
