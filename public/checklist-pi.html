Critical steps to protect your rights after an accident in Florida

0 completed

At the Scene / Immediately After
Safety and Evidence
✓
Call 911 — get a police report or incident report filed
A police report is one of the strongest pieces of evidence. Do not skip this.
✓
Seek medical attention immediately — even if you feel fine
You have only 14 days to seek treatment to preserve PIP benefits (F.S. 627.736).
✓
Photograph the scene — vehicle damage, road conditions, your injuries
✓
Get names, phone numbers, and insurance info from ALL parties
✓
Get contact information from witnesses
✓
Do NOT admit fault or apologize at the scene
Even "I'm sorry" can be twisted into an admission of liability.
✓
Do NOT discuss the accident on social media
Defense lawyers and insurance adjusters monitor Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok.
✓
Report the accident to your own insurance (keep it brief and factual)
In the First 72 Hours
Medical Care
✓
Follow up with your doctor or urgent care within 72 hours
If you waited to see a doctor, insurers argue you weren't really hurt.
✓
Tell EVERY doctor your injuries are from the accident
If records don't connect injuries to the accident, the insurer will deny the claim.
✓
Follow ALL prescribed treatment — PT, specialists, medications
Gaps in treatment are the #1 way insurance companies reduce claim values.
✓
Start a daily pain journal — rate pain 1-10, note limitations
This becomes powerful evidence of pain and suffering at settlement or trial.
✓
Save ALL medical bills and receipts — even copays and pharmacy
Protect Your Claim
✓
Do NOT give a recorded statement to the other driver's insurance
They are not on your side. Anything you say will be used to minimize your payout.
✓
Do NOT sign any documents from the other party's insurer
Signing a medical authorization gives them access to your ENTIRE medical history.
✓
Do NOT accept a quick settlement offer
Insurance companies rush settlements before you know the full extent of your injuries.
✓
Track all lost wages — get a letter from your employer
✓
Document how the injury affects daily life, hobbies, family
Know Your Rights — Florida Law
Critical Deadlines
✓
Statute of limitations: 2 years from date of injury (F.S. 95.11)
Miss this deadline and you lose your right to sue — permanently.
✓
PIP benefits: 14 days to seek treatment (F.S. 627.736)
✓
51%+ at fault = recover NOTHING under modified comparative negligence
Florida's 2023 tort reform. Having an attorney is more important than ever.
✓
You can recover: medical bills, lost wages, pain & suffering, future care
✓
PI lawyers work on contingency — no upfront cost to you
