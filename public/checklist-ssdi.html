SSDI Approval Checklist

Everything you need to file and win your Social Security Disability claim

Before You Apply
Eligibility Check
✓
Confirm your medical condition prevents you from working
The SSA requires your condition to last at least 12 months or be terminal.
✓
Verify you have enough work credits (typically 40 credits, 20 in last 10 years)
Check your Social Security Statement at ssa.gov/myaccount for your exact credits.
✓
Confirm earnings are below SGA ($1,620/month in 2026)
Even part-time work above this threshold will result in automatic denial.
✓
Identify your condition in the SSA Blue Book listings
If your condition matches a Blue Book listing, approval is significantly faster.
Medical Evidence to Gather
✓
Complete medical records from all treating physicians (last 2+ years)
Request records NOW — doctor offices can take 2-4 weeks to process.
✓
List of all medications with dosages and prescribing doctors
✓
Lab results, imaging (MRI, X-ray, CT), and test results
✓
Mental health treatment records (therapist, psychiatrist) if applicable
Mental health conditions are among the most commonly approved — document everything.
✓
Hospital admission and discharge summaries
✓
Doctor's statement about functional limitations (RFC form)
This is the single most important document. Ask your doctor to be specific about what you CANNOT do.
✓
List of all medical providers with addresses and phone numbers
Personal Documentation
✓
Social Security number and birth certificate
✓
Proof of U.S. citizenship or lawful residency
✓
W-2 forms or tax returns (last 2 years)
✓
Workers' comp or other disability benefit information
✓
Completed Function Report (SSA-3373) — describe your WORST days
Be honest but thorough. Describe how your condition affects daily activities in detail.
✓
Work history report (SSA-3369) for last 15 years
During the Process
After Filing
✓
Keep copies of everything you submit to the SSA
✓
Attend all consultative exams (CE) if scheduled
Missing a CE is grounds for automatic denial.
✓
Continue all medical treatment — no gaps
The SSA will argue: if you're not treating, you must not be that disabled.
✓
Do NOT post physical activities on social media
SSA investigators check Facebook, Instagram, and TikTok.
✓
Track symptoms and bad days in a daily journal
✓
Respond to all SSA correspondence within deadlines
If Denied
✓
File appeal within 60 days — DO NOT MISS THIS DEADLINE
After 60 days, you must start a brand new application from scratch.
✓
Request a hearing before an Administrative Law Judge (ALJ)
Claimants with attorneys win at the ALJ hearing level at significantly higher rates.
✓
Gather new medical evidence not in your original application
✓
Consider hiring a disability attorney (no fee unless you win)
